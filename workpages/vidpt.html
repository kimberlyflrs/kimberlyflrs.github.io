VidPt

A mobile application that engages users in their physical therapy journey.

Project:

Physical Therapy Application - Senior Capstone Project

Sponser:

Michael Bise - Founder of VidPT

Team:

Shirby Wang, Amanda Hernandez, Nickolas Saba

Role:

Front-End Code and UX/UI Design

Duration:

6 months(September 2018-March 2019)

Platform:

Mobile Application

Problem Statement

Going through physical therapy can be a lonely and long process. The person can feel unmotivated and discouraged from the complicated paper instructions given to them. There may be little support for them.

VidPT aims to help users conquer their physical therapy with informative exercise videos, progress tracking, and a supportive community. It aims to help users build positive habits towards recovery.

Project Goals

1. Learn: Allow users to know about their current injury status.
2. Build: Allow users to have a personalized yet customizable routine.
3. Measure: Be able to track progress such as pain levels and strength.
4. Rally: Interaction amongst users for motivation.
5. Compete: Allow users to compete with one another in their final stage of recovery.

Process

The process followed an agile method were we designed and coded to our flexible requirements. The team met with Michael periodically to discuss the requirements of the application. We discussed the specific requirements and separated them into functional and non-functional requirements. We created a use case diagram and UML diagram to further understand what needed to be done.

Interviews were conducted with former physical therapy users and a physical therapist. Personas were made to understand the user base. We made sure all of the workouts were approved and reviewed the HIPPA law. A disclaimer was added in order for users to understand that this is not a replacement for treatment but a supplementary tool.

Front-End Development

The team used the React framework to create the mobile application. I translated the sketches into the user interface and incorporated node packages to build the progress and survey components.

Final MVP

Learn

When users first sign up, they take a quick survey to pinpoint their injury status. The answers are then processed through an algorithm that will select the best routine for our user from the database.

Measure

Our users can see their progress through the graph and individual entries. Progress that is tracked is performance and range of motion. This information comes from the initial questionnaire and a progress survey that is given after every x amount of workouts.

Build

Users are given a set of exercises they can pick from according to their current stage. They can formulate a six exercise routine that contains the respective amount of reps/sets, video, and step by step instructions.

Compete/Rally

Users earn points after each workout which advances their rank. If users are consistent in their routine, they will start earning a streak. In the future, users will be able to share their experiences through a community section.
