Food Journal

An application that supports logging food through multiple modalities.

Project:

MultiModal Food App - Research Project

Team:

Professor Daniel Epstein and Yuqi Huai

Role:

Front-End Code and UX/UI Design

Duration:

6 months(January 2019-June 2019)

Platform:

Mobile Application

Problem Statement

Understand which logging method users tend to pick when logging food and why they lean towards that option.

Project Goals

1. Create a Mobile Application for IOS
2. Create an interface that allows easy access to logging
3. Code for Amazon Alexa and Google Home Devices
4. Connect Voice Assistance devices to Firebase

Sketches and Wireframes

Previous Next

The goal of the project is to understand which logging method people pick and why. The modalities we included were logging a chunk of text, adding an image, voice recording, a url, searching a database, and scanning a barcode. It was important to make the logging process for each option as easy as possible. I started off by sketching different main log pages with the mentioned modality. For example, the camera, bar code, and the voice recording will leverage from the phones existing capabilities.

Voice Assistance

The team decided to incorporate Amazon Alexa and Google Home Devices to the research. I used the Alexa Voice Service and DialogFlow developer environment to integrate the Firebase Database. Users can open the app on each device and say “Log (Food Name)” after signing in to their account.

Front-End Development

The team used the Ionic framework to create the mobile application. I was able to take my designs and translate them to code through html and css. I incorporated interactions such as exploring previous entries through a pop up menu and worked on the log in and log out system thanks to javascript.
